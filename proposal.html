Project Scope & User Journey

Our approach to building the MVP features

1. Smart Onboarding & Plan Generation

The first interaction sets the tone. We will build a seamless flow where users don't just "sign up" but "start a journey".


  • 1 User Profiling: Collect crucial data: Age, Experience, Recent race times, and Goal (Distance + Target Date).
  • 2 Assessment: Integration of an initial "Baseline Run" or manual entry to calibrate the user's current fitness.
  • 3 The "Magic Moment": The system generates the first week's training plan instantly. Users see exactly what they need to do next.

2. Core Running & Tracking Engine

The app must be a capable tracker to replace Strava/Nike Run Club for the user's daily needs.


  • 4 Accurate GPS Tracking: Real-time tracking of Distance, Pace, Time, and Route mapping.
  • 5 Context-Aware Modes:
    • Base Run: Just run. The system logs it to build volume.
    • Training Run: Guided run based on today's assigned workout (e.g., "Run 5km at Easy Pace").
  • 6 Feedback Loop (RPE): Post-run survey asks: "How hard was that? (1-10)". This data point is critical for the AI adaptation engine.

3. Engagement & Ecosystem

Features that keep the user coming back.


  • 7 Google Calendar Sync: The training plan automatically syncs to the user's personal calendar for daily reminders.
  • 8 Leaderboards: GPS-validated leaderboards (City/State level) based on distance consistency, not just speed.
  • 9 Weekly Review: Every Sunday, the system presents a summary of the week and "unlocks" the next week's adapted plan.
